# lululemon Valuation Story

## Core Story

lululemon is no longer a hyper-growth apparel brand, but it remains a high-quality premium retailer.

The market appears to be pricing in a severe slowdown because Americas growth has weakened and comparable sales are negative. Our base case assumes a mature-growth transition: modest North America performance, continued international expansion, partial margin recovery, disciplined reinvestment, and a more normal risk profile for a premium discretionary retail company.

The valuation tests whether the current market price overreacts to short-term weakness or correctly reflects a lower long-term growth profile.

## Evidence From Current Data

The story is supported by the data collected from SEC filings:

- Americas revenue declined by 1% in fiscal 2025 versus fiscal 2024.
- Americas comparable sales declined by 3% in fiscal 2025 versus fiscal 2024.
- China Mainland revenue grew by 29% reported and 28% in constant currency.
- Rest of World revenue grew by 16% reported and 14% in constant currency.
- Total comparable sales grew only 2%, showing that international strength is partly offset by Americas weakness.
- Operating margin fell from 23.7% in fiscal 2024 to 19.9% in fiscal 2025.
- Inventory increased to 15.3% of revenue in fiscal 2025, compared with 13.6% in fiscal 2024.

## Story To Numbers

### Growth

lululemon should not be modeled as a hyper-growth company anymore. Americas is large and currently weak, while international markets remain smaller but faster-growing.

Base case implication:

- Near-term revenue growth should be modest.
- Growth can improve over the medium term if international expansion continues.
- Long-term growth should fade toward a mature premium retail profile.

### Profitability

lululemon still has premium brand economics, but the recent margin compression should not be ignored. Competition, product mix, promotions, tariffs, and inventory markdown risk can limit full recovery to prior peak margins.

Base case implication:

- Operating margin partially recovers from the fiscal 2025 level.
- Base margin should not assume an automatic return to the fiscal 2024 peak.

### Reinvestment

International expansion, new stores, supply chain capacity, technology, and inventory require reinvestment. lululemon is asset-light compared with manufacturers, but it is not a software business.

Base case implication:

- Sales-to-capital should be lower than the first-pass optimistic model.
- Reinvestment should remain meaningful as the company grows internationally.

### Risk

lululemon is exposed to discretionary consumer spending, fashion cycles, brand heat, tariffs, international execution, and China-related risk. A mature premium retailer should have a higher cost of capital than a low-risk consumer staple.

Base case implication:

- WACC should be higher than the first-pass model.
- Terminal WACC should not fall too aggressively.

### Terminal State

In the terminal period, lululemon should be treated as a mature global premium apparel retailer, not a hyper-growth platform.

Base case implication:

- Terminal growth should be close to long-term inflation / mature economy growth.
- Terminal operating margin should remain strong but not heroic.

## Calibrated Scenario Logic

### Bear Case

North America weakness persists, international growth slows, margin pressure remains, and reinvestment efficiency declines.

This case approximates a pessimistic market narrative.

### Base Case

North America stabilizes at low growth, international markets continue to grow but gradually slow, margin partially recovers, and the company remains a high-quality mature premium retailer.

This is the main valuation story.

### Bull Case

International expansion remains strong, brand heat improves, Americas stabilizes faster than expected, and operating leverage improves.

This case is possible but should not be treated as the central estimate.

## Assumption Changes From First-Pass Model

The first-pass model was useful for testing the mechanics, but it was too optimistic because it assumed stronger growth recovery, lower risk, and higher capital efficiency.

The calibrated model should:

- Lower base revenue growth assumptions.
- Lower base target operating margin.
- Lower base sales-to-capital.
- Increase base WACC.
- Lower base terminal growth.
- Keep the bull case optimistic but less heroic.
