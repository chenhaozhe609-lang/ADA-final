## Relevant Topics For This Project

### Slides 1 - Accounting Foundations

Useful for basic financial statement language and accounting assumptions:

- Financial statements.
- Accounting principles and assumptions.
- Statement of cash flows.
- Matching principle and comparability.

Potential report use:

- Explain why the valuation starts from audited financial statements.
- Explain the role of income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow statement data.

- Operating cycle for a merchandiser.
- Inventory systems.
- Merchandise purchases.
- Inventory costing and financial statement effects.
- Goods damaged or obsolete.

Potential report use:

### Slides 3 - Discounted Cash Flow Valuation

Most directly relevant to the valuation model:

- Future value and compounding.
- Present value and discounting.
- Net present value.
- Multiple cash flows.
- Perpetuity and growing perpetuity.
- Annuity and growing annuity.
- Firm value as the present value of firm cash flows.
- Estimating future cash flows and discount rates.

Potential report use:

- Explain the DCF/NPV framework.
- Explain why the fair value estimate depends on cash flow size, timing, and risk.
- Support terminal value logic using growing perpetuity concepts.

### Slides 4 - Risk, Return, CAPM, WACC, And Capital Structure

Highly relevant to the discount-rate and equity-bridge parts of the valuation:

- Dollar return and percentage return.
- Historical returns and risk premium.
- Variance, standard deviation, covariance, correlation, and diversification.
- CAPM cost of equity: risk-free rate, market risk premium, and beta.
- Beta estimation caveats: beta changes over time, sample size can be inadequate, and beta is affected by business risk and financial leverage.
- WACC formula using equity weight, debt weight, cost of equity, after-tax cost of debt, and tax rate.
- Firm valuation: firm value is the present value of expected cash flows discounted at WACC; equity value is obtained by subtracting debt from firm value.
- Capital structure concepts and the relationship between debt, equity, leverage, firm value, and shareholder value.

Potential report use:

- Strengthen the explanation of why WACC is the appropriate discount rate for FCFF.
- Add course-aligned support for the risk assumptions in bear/base/bull scenarios.
- Explain that a fully developed WACC would normally use CAPM cost of equity: `Cost of Equity = Risk-free Rate + Beta × Market Risk Premium`.
- Support our equity bridge from enterprise value to equity value by subtracting debt-like claims.
- Justify why the report should discuss beta, risk premium, and capital structure as limitations or future refinements.

Impact on current project:

- No direct conflict with the current DCF structure. Our FCFF-to-enterprise-value-to-equity-value approach is consistent with the slides.
- The main gap is that our current WACC is scenario-calibrated rather than fully built from CAPM inputs. This should remain a limitation or next-step refinement unless we collect beta, risk-free rate, market risk premium, and peer leverage evidence.
- Treating operating lease liabilities as debt-like claims is directionally consistent with the course's firm-value-to-equity-value logic, but the final report should state it as a modeling judgment.
- The slides make Monte Carlo less necessary, not more necessary. They emphasize risk, expected return, beta, WACC, and scenario risk rather than simulation.
