# Valuation Report Outline

## 1. Executive Summary

- Selected company and valuation date.
- One-sentence investment story.
- Estimated fair value per share.
- Actual market price on the valuation date.
- Overvalued / undervalued / fairly valued conclusion.
- Main reason for the valuation gap.

## 2. Company Overview

- Business model.
- Main products or services.
- Revenue segments.
- Geographic exposure.
- Recent strategic developments.

## 3. Industry Analysis

- Industry size and growth.
- Key demand drivers.
- Competitive structure.
- Profitability and cyclicality.
- Regulatory, macro, or technology risks.
- How industry conditions affect the selected company.

## 4. Historical Fundamentals

- Revenue growth.
- Gross margin and operating margin.
- R&D or capital expenditure intensity.
- Working capital needs.
- Debt, cash, and balance sheet strength.
- Return on invested capital.
- Comparison with major peers.

## 5. Valuation Story

Translate the qualitative analysis into a concise valuation story:

- Main story: lululemon is transitioning from hyper-growth to mature premium retail, while the market may be overreacting to short-term Americas weakness.
- Growth: why revenue will grow, slow, or decline.
- Profitability: why margins will improve, remain stable, or compress.
- Reinvestment: how much capital the company needs to support growth.
- Risk: why the discount rate should be high or low.
- Terminal state: what the company looks like after the high-growth period.
- Story-to-numbers bridge: explain how each part of the story changes the DCF assumptions.
- First-pass model calibration: briefly explain why the first-pass DCF was too optimistic and how the calibrated assumptions were adjusted.

## 6. DCF Valuation Methodology

- FCFF formula.
- Forecast horizon.
- Revenue forecast method.
- Operating margin forecast method.
- Tax assumptions.
- Reinvestment assumptions.
- Cost of capital assumptions.
- Terminal value method.
- Equity value bridge.

## 7. Base Case Valuation

- Forecast table.
- Free cash flow table.
- Calibrated assumption table.
- Enterprise value.
- Equity value.
- Fair value per share.
- Comparison with actual price.

## 8. Scenario and Sensitivity Analysis

- Bear case.
- Base case.
- Bull case.
- Sensitivity table for key assumptions.
- Optional simulation result.
- Main value drivers.

## 9. Discussion of Market Price Gap

If estimated value differs significantly from market price:

- Explain what the market may be assuming.
- Explain what the model may be missing.
- Discuss whether the market price can converge to the model value.
- Discuss risks to the conclusion.

## 10. Conclusion

- Final valuation conclusion.
- Most important assumption.
- Final recommendation or judgment.

## Appendix

- Data sources.
- Detailed financial tables.
- Formula explanation.
- AI usage summary reference.
- Code and Excel file reference.
