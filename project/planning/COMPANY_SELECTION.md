# Company Selection Decision

## Final Choice

The final target company is **lululemon athletica inc. (LULU)**.

## Why lululemon Fits the Project

lululemon is a strong fit for this valuation project because it combines accessible public financial data with a clear and debatable business story. It is large enough to have mature disclosures and analyst attention, but its future value still depends on assumptions that can be debated and quantified.

The company is also easier to explain than many technology or financial firms. A reader who has never done valuation can understand the business model: lululemon designs and sells premium athletic apparel through company-operated stores and direct-to-consumer channels.

## Main Valuation Question

Is lululemon a mature premium retail brand facing a structural slowdown, or is the market underestimating its international growth potential, brand strength, and long-term margin resilience?

## Why It Is Better Than Hermes for This Course Project

Hermes is a distinctive and high-quality company, but its valuation story depends heavily on luxury scarcity, craftsmanship, family control, and very high pricing power. That story is interesting, but it can become abstract and harder to translate into a beginner-friendly DCF model.

lululemon is more operationally transparent. Its valuation can be tied directly to store count, regional growth, comparable sales, channel mix, gross margin, inventory management, operating leverage, reinvestment, and share repurchases. This makes it easier to build a replicable case-study report and a useful code-driven dashboard.

## Core Story Hypotheses

The base case will likely assume:

- North America growth slows from the company's earlier high-growth period.
- International markets, especially outside North America, remain the main growth driver.
- Gross margin stays strong but may face pressure from promotions, product mix, tariffs, or inventory issues.
- Operating margin remains above typical apparel retailers, but does not expand indefinitely.
- Reinvestment continues through store expansion, product development, technology, and working capital.
- Share repurchases may support per-share value but should not replace operating value creation.

## Key Risks to Analyze

- Competition from Alo Yoga, Vuori, Nike, Adidas, Athleta, and other premium athleisure brands.
- Fashion risk and product newness risk.
- North America saturation.
- Inventory markdown risk.
- Tariff and sourcing cost pressure.
- Consumer discretionary weakness.
- International execution risk.

## Data Needed Next

- Annual reports / 10-K filings for at least five fiscal years.
- Latest 10-Q or trailing twelve month figures.
- Revenue by geography.
- Revenue by channel, if disclosed.
- Store count and store openings.
- Gross margin, operating margin, net margin.
- Inventory and working capital.
- Cash, debt, lease liabilities, share count, and share repurchases.
- Current market price and market capitalization on the chosen valuation date.
- Peer set and industry data for athletic apparel and premium retail.
